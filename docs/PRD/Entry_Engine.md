# 15. Entry Engine

---

# Overview

The Entry Engine is the final decision-making engine before capital is committed to the market.

Every previous engine exists to progressively eliminate poor-quality opportunities.

The Entry Engine does **not** search for trading opportunities.

Instead, it validates whether every prerequisite generated by upstream engines is simultaneously satisfied.

Only after every mandatory validation succeeds should the Entry Engine approve a trade.

The Entry Engine represents the transition point between:

Market Analysis

↓

Trading Decision

Unlike retail trading systems that often enter positions based on one or two indicators, the Institutional Swing Trading Platform requires confirmation from multiple independent analytical engines before approving capital deployment.

No trade should ever bypass this engine.

---

# Objective

The Entry Engine answers one final question.

> **"Does this opportunity satisfy every requirement necessary to justify risking capital?"**

If the answer is:

No

↓

Reject Trade

If the answer is:

Yes

↓

Forward the opportunity to the Risk Engine.

The Entry Engine itself never calculates position size.

It never places orders.

It only decides whether a trade deserves further consideration.

---

# Core Philosophy

The Entry Engine follows one permanent rule:

> **Every approved trade must satisfy 100% of mandatory conditions.**

There is no concept of:

- Almost Valid
- Nearly Correct
- Close Enough
- High Confidence Despite Missing Rules

Trading decisions remain deterministic.

Every mandatory rule must pass.

# Entry Approval Philosophy

Retail traders often ask:

"Should I buy this stock?"

The platform asks a different question.

"Has the market objectively earned permission for us to risk capital?"

This difference appears small,

but fundamentally changes decision quality.

The platform never forces trades.

The market must satisfy every predefined condition.

---

# Mandatory Validation Checklist

Every trade must satisfy all mandatory validations.

Failure of a single rule immediately rejects the trade.

The Entry Engine performs no partial approvals.

---

# Validation 1 — Market Validation

Before analyzing an individual stock,

the broader market should remain favorable.

The Entry Engine requests the latest Market Status from the Market Intelligence Engine.

Possible outputs:

- Bullish
- Neutral
- Bearish
- High Risk
- Restricted

If market conditions prohibit new trades,

the opportunity is rejected regardless of stock quality.

---

# Validation 2 — Universe Validation

The stock must still belong to the approved Market Universe.

The engine verifies:

- Active Listing
- Eligible Exchange
- Trading Active
- Historical Data Valid

Any inconsistency immediately invalidates the setup.

---

# Validation 3 — Watchlist Validation

The stock must currently exist inside the active watchlist.

If removed during the latest refresh,

the setup becomes invalid.

This prevents trades based on outdated analysis.

---

# Validation 4 — Trend Validation

The Trend Engine must report:

- Confirmed Trend
- Positive EMA Structure
- Trend Eligible

Trend deterioration immediately cancels the opportunity.

---

# Validation 5 — Consolidation Validation

A valid consolidation object must exist.

The Entry Engine verifies:

- Consolidation Status
- Support
- Resistance
- Duration
- Quality Score

Without consolidation,

there can be no liquidity grab.

---

# Validation 6 — Liquidity Grab Validation

A fully validated Liquidity Grab Object must exist.

Required information includes:

- Breakdown
- Recovery
- Volume Confirmation
- Recovery Duration
- Quality Score

No liquidity grab,

No trade.

This is the defining characteristic of the strategy.

---

# Validation 7 — Recovery Completion

The recovery must be complete.

Incomplete recoveries remain under observation.

Example

```
Support Break

↓

Recovery Started

↓

Still Below Support

↓

Reject
```

Only complete recovery qualifies.

---

# Validation 8 — Closing Price Confirmation

The platform evaluates completed daily candles only.

Intraday movement is ignored.

Reason:

Daily closes provide significantly more reliable confirmation than temporary intraday movements.

The Entry Engine never enters positions based upon incomplete candles.

---

# Validation 9 — Volume Confirmation

The abnormal volume observed during recovery must remain valid.

The Entry Engine verifies:

- Volume Ratio
- Relative Participation
- Volume Integrity

If abnormal participation disappears,

the trade is rejected.

---

# Validation 10 — Data Integrity

Before approving a trade,

the engine performs a final integrity check.

Examples include:

- Missing Data
- Duplicate Candles
- Incorrect Corporate Action Adjustment
- Corrupted Market Data

Trading should never occur using questionable data.

---

# Entry Timing

The platform deliberately avoids entering immediately after the support break.

Instead,

the sequence remains:

```
Consolidation

↓

Support Break

↓

Recovery

↓

Close Back Inside Consolidation

↓

Volume Confirmation

↓

Entry Approved
```

This delay is intentional.

It reduces participation in failed breakdowns.

---

# Entry Price

The Entry Engine does **not** attempt to optimize entry price.

Instead,

it defines the official Entry Price object.

Possible future execution methods include:

- Market Order
- Limit Order
- Closing Price Execution
- Next Session Opening Execution

The execution mechanism remains configurable.

However,

the analytical approval remains identical.

# Rejection Reasons

Every rejected opportunity must record its rejection reason.

Examples include:

- Weak Market
- Trend Failure
- Consolidation Invalid
- Recovery Failed
- Volume Insufficient
- Watchlist Removed
- Data Corrupted
- Configuration Conflict

Rejections should never disappear.

Historical rejection data provides valuable research opportunities.

# Configuration

Configurable parameters include:

- Entry Execution Mode
- Entry Confirmation Delay
- Daily Close Requirement
- Minimum Liquidity Grab Score
- Minimum Trend Score
- Minimum Consolidation Score
- Minimum Market Score
- Strategy Version

Configuration updates should never modify historical trade records.

Every trade must reference the configuration version used during approval.

---

# Expected Output

The Entry Engine produces only one of two outcomes.

```
Trade Approved
```

or

```
Trade Rejected
```

It intentionally performs no:

- Position Sizing
- Stop Loss Calculation
- Portfolio Analysis
- Broker Communication

These responsibilities belong to downstream engines.

Maintaining strict separation of responsibilities improves maintainability, auditability, and testing while preventing unintended coupling between analytical decision-making and execution.
